Welcome to Alexandria's Historical Places

Alexandria's Landmarks

Qaitbay Fort

The Citadel of Qaitbay is a 15th-century defensive fortress located on the Mediterranean sea coast. It was built from 1477 to 1479 by Sultan Al-Ashraf Sayf al-Din Qa'it Bay.

Learn More
Montaza Palace

Montaza Palace (Asr el Montaza) is a palace, museum and extensive gardens in the Montaza district of Alexandria. Palace is overlooking a beach on the Mediterranean Sea.

Learn More
National Museum

The museum is a unique architectural masterpiece, and was originally a palace for Asaad Basili, it was converted into a museum, to include a collection of artifacts.

Learn More
Ancient Roman Theater

The Roman Theatre has marble seating for seven to eight hundred, cruder galleries for the plebs, and a forecourt with two patches of mosaic flooring. It was discovered by accident in 1960.

Learn More
Bibliotheca Alexandria

The Bibliotheca Alexandria is a major library and cultural center on the shore of the Mediterranean Sea. It contains different books in classical Arabic, English, and French languages.

Learn More
Royal Jewelry Museum

The Royal Jewelry Museum displays the collectibles of the Egyptian royal family, founded by Muhammad Ali Pasha in 1805. It uniquely merges between European and Islamic styles.
